About Me

I’m a student. I happen to be in college, but more importantly, I find an innumerable plethora of things interesting. For example, some activities that I’ve really enjoyed include the following: programming, building large things out of wood, creating electrical circuits, growing mammalian cells in a petri dish, learning about the human brain, crafting, making different kinds of art, and writing novels. My specialty with finding an interest in things lends me to being a prolific problem-solver.


Life is made up of problems to solve, and I love resolving them. The majority of activities I enjoy are hands-on for that reason. When one is dealing with a concept or theory, the only space they have to work on problems is a mental space, which is designed specifically to house that problem. Physical spaces are rarely designed and designated only for one activity. This prompts the possible integration of unorthodox objects in a solution. Physical activities also can create problems that wouldn’t pop up in a mental space. The physicality of these activities allows me to come up with a solution that is resourceful, clever, or even elegant. I love taking “okay” things and polishing them so they shine. As such, I find these “solutions+” to be extra rewarding.


Some of the activities I involve myself in are more daunting than usual. Looking at a list of things I pour my soul into, they are all things where I am largely autonomous, have become a leader, and can be intensely stressful at times. I think these activities (scorekeeping for FIRST events, leading a robotics team, and running lights for theatre productions have become my wheelhouse because I can “hoard” knowledge in them, and in doing so, become the best person to lead.


In conclusion, I am a person who loves to use her brain and many interests to create novel solutions.
